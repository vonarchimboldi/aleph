July 6: Sequences and Recurrences

A one-hour Competition Math session on recurrence structure. The target habit is to compute a few terms, identify whether the recurrence is arithmetic, geometric, linear, or telescoping, and then prove the pattern.

Core Pattern

For a recurrence, first decide what kind of step transforms one term into the next. Linear recurrences use characteristic equations; telescoping recurrences collapse after summing differences.

\[a_{n+2}=pa_{n+1}+qa_n\quad\Longrightarrow\quad r^2=pr+q.\]

One-Hour Workflow

  • Spend 8 minutes classifying recurrence types.
  • Spend 30 minutes on Problems 1-6.
  • Spend 15 minutes on Problems 7-10.
  • Write the first five terms before guessing.
  • For linear recurrences, try the characteristic equation.
  • For sums of differences, look for telescoping.
Problems 1-5 - Concept Builders
1. Arithmetic Recurrence Check
ConceptArithmetic

If \(a_1=7\) and \(a_{n+1}=a_n+4\), find \(a_{20}\).

Solution

This is arithmetic with common difference 4. The formula is \(a_n=7+4(n-1)\). Hence \(a_{20}=7+76=83\).

2. Geometric Recurrence Check
ConceptGeometric

If \(b_1=3\) and \(b_{n+1}=2b_n\), find \(b_8\).

Solution

Each step multiplies by 2, so \(b_n=3\cdot2^{n-1}\). Therefore \(b_8=3\cdot2^7=384\).

3. Telescoping Difference
ConceptTelescoping

Suppose \(a_1=5\) and \(a_{n+1}-a_n=2n\). Find \(a_6\).

Solution

Sum the differences from \(n=1\) to 5: \(a_6-a_1=2(1+2+3+4+5)=30\). Thus \(a_6=35\).

4. Fibonacci-Style First Terms
ConceptSecond order

Let \(c_1=1\), \(c_2=3\), and \(c_{n+2}=c_{n+1}+c_n\). Find \(c_6\).

Solution

Compute terms: \(c_3=4\), \(c_4=7\), \(c_5=11\), and \(c_6=18\).

5. Characteristic Root Warmup
ConceptCharacteristic

For \(a_{n+2}=3a_{n+1}-2a_n\), write the characteristic equation and factor it.

Solution

Try \(a_n=r^n\). Then \(r^{n+2}=3r^{n+1}-2r^n\), so \(r^2-3r+2=0\). This factors as \((r-1)(r-2)=0\).

Problems 6-8 - Pattern Integration
6. Solve a Two-Root Recurrence
IntegrationLinear recurrence

Let \(a_0=1\), \(a_1=2\), and \(a_{n+2}=3a_{n+1}-2a_n\). Find a formula for \(a_n\).

Solution

The characteristic roots are 1 and 2, so \(a_n=A+B2^n\). From \(a_0=1\), \(A+B=1\). From \(a_1=2\), \(A+2B=2\). Thus \(B=1\), \(A=0\), and \(a_n=2^n\).

7. Telescoping Reciprocal Difference
IntegrationPartial fractions

Compute \(\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{k(k+1)}\).

Solution

Decompose \(\frac{1}{k(k+1)}=\frac1k-\frac1{k+1}\). The sum telescopes to \(1-\frac1{n+1}=\frac{n}{n+1}\).

8. Recurrence With Constant Term
IntegrationShift

Let \(a_1=1\) and \(a_{n+1}=2a_n+3\). Compute \(a_4\).

Solution

Compute carefully: \(a_2=2(1)+3=5\), \(a_3=2(5)+3=13\), and \(a_4=2(13)+3=29\). The constant term means this is not purely geometric.

Problems 9-10 - Challenge Problems
9. AIME-Style Recurrence Value
ChallengeFast iteration

A sequence satisfies \(x_0=0\) and \(x_{n+1}=x_n+2n+1\). Find \(x_{20}\).

Solution

The difference \(x_{n+1}-x_n=2n+1\). Sum from \(n=0\) to 19: \(x_{20}=\sum_{n=0}^{19}(2n+1)\). This is the sum of the first 20 odd numbers, so \(x_{20}=20^2=400\).

10. Recurrence Recognition Challenge
ChallengeCharacteristic

Let \(a_0=2\), \(a_1=5\), and \(a_{n+2}=4a_{n+1}-4a_n\). Find \(a_3\) and state why the characteristic equation has a repeated root.

Solution

First compute directly: \(a_2=4(5)-4(2)=12\), and \(a_3=4(12)-4(5)=28\). The characteristic equation is \(r^2-4r+4=0=(r-2)^2\), so the root 2 is repeated.
