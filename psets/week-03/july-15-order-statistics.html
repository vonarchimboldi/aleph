Core Pattern

For iid continuous samples, min and max are easiest through CDF events. The \(k\)th order statistic is controlled by a binomial count.

\[P(X_{(k)}\le x)=\sum_{j=k}^n\binom nj F(x)^j(1-F(x))^{n-j}.\]

Setup Checklist

  • For a maximum, use all observations \(\le x\).
  • For a minimum, use at least one observation \(\le x\) or all observations \(>x\).
  • For \(X_{(k)}\), count how many observations are at most \(x\).
  • Write support before integrating.
  • For uniforms, remember \(U_{(k)}\sim\operatorname{Beta}(k,n+1-k)\).
  • For spacings, decide whether a count method is simpler than a density.
Problems 1-5 - Concept Builders
1. Maximum of four uniforms
ConceptMax

Let \(U_1,\ldots,U_4\) be iid Uniform\((0,1)\). Find \(P(U_{(4)}\le x)\) for \(0\le x\le1\).

Solution

The maximum is at most \(x\) exactly when all four observations are at most \(x\). Thus \(P(U_{(4)}\le x)=x^4\).

2. Minimum of five uniforms
ConceptMin

Let \(U_1,\ldots,U_5\) be iid Uniform\((0,1)\). Find \(P(U_{(1)}>x)\) for \(0\le x\le1\).

Solution

The minimum is greater than \(x\) when all five observations exceed \(x\). The probability is \((1-x)^5\).

3. Median CDF from a binomial count
ConceptMedian

For five iid Uniform\((0,1)\) observations, express \(P(U_{(3)}\le x)\).

Solution

The third order statistic is at most \(x\) when at least three observations are at most \(x\). Thus \(P(U_{(3)}\le x)=\sum_{j=3}^5\binom5j x^j(1-x)^{5-j}\).

5. Expected maximum shortcut
ConceptExpectation

For \(n\) iid Uniform\((0,1)\) variables, state \(E[U_{(n)}]\).

Solution

\(U_{(n)}\sim\operatorname{Beta}(n,1)\). Its mean is \(n/(n+1)\).

Problems 6-8 - Application Problems
6. Range below one half
ApplicationRange

Three iid Uniform\((0,1)\) points are sampled. Compute the probability that all points lie inside some interval of length \(1/2\) whose left endpoint is the minimum sample value.

Solution

Let the minimum be \(m\). Given the minimum is \(m\), the other two points must lie in \([m,m+1/2]\). A direct ordered-density calculation gives \(P(U_{(3)}-U_{(1)}\le1/2)=3(1/2)^2-2(1/2)^3=1/2\).

7. First arrival among exponential clocks
ApplicationExponential

If \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) are iid exponential with rate \(\lambda\), find the distribution of \(X_{(1)}\).

Solution

\(P(X_{(1)}>t)=P(X_1>t,\ldots,X_n>t)=e^{-n\lambda t}\). Therefore \(X_{(1)}\) is exponential with rate \(n\lambda\).

8. Third-smallest threshold count
ApplicationBinomial count

Seven iid observations have CDF \(F\). Express \(P(X_{(3)}>a)\).

Solution

The third-smallest value is greater than \(a\) when at most two observations are at most \(a\). Hence \(P(X_{(3)}>a)=\sum_{j=0}^2\binom7j F(a)^j(1-F(a))^{7-j}\).

Problems 9-10 - ISI-Style Challenge
9. Median below one half
ISI-styleMedian

For five iid Uniform\((0,1)\) observations, compute \(P(U_{(3)}\le1/2)\).

Solution

At least three of five points must be at most \(1/2\). The binomial probability is \(\sum_{j=3}^5\binom5j(1/2)^5=16/32=1/2\).
