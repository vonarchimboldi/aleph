Core Pattern

Compute the inner average after information is known, then average that result.

\[E[X]=E[E[X\mid Y]],\qquad \operatorname{Var}(X)=E[\operatorname{Var}(X\mid Y)]+\operatorname{Var}(E[X\mid Y]).\]

Setup Checklist

  • Name the conditioning variable.
  • Write \(E[X\mid Y=y]\) before averaging.
  • Keep \(E[X\mid Y]\) random until the outer expectation.
  • Use total variance when the question asks for spread.
  • Check conditional support.
  • Use random-sum formulas only after stating the conditioning step.
Problems 1-5 - Concept Builders
1. Reward after a chosen box
ConceptTower

A box \(Y\) is A with probability \(1/4\) and B with probability \(3/4\). The mean reward is 12 from A and 4 from B. Compute \(E[X]\).

Solution

Condition on \(Y\). \(E[X\mid Y=A]=12\) and \(E[X\mid Y=B]=4\). Thus \(E[X]=(1/4)12+(3/4)4=6\).

2. Conditional mean from a table
ConceptGrouped average

\(Y\) equals 0 or 1 with probabilities \(1/2,1/2\). Given \(Y=0\), \(E[X\mid Y=0]=3\). Given \(Y=1\), \(E[X\mid Y=1]=9\). Find \(E[E[X\mid Y]]\).

Solution

The random variable \(E[X\mid Y]\) equals 3 or 9 with equal probability. Its expectation is \((3+9)/2=6\), which equals \(E[X]\).

3. Random number of Bernoulli trials
ConceptRandom sum

Given \(N\), let \(X\mid N\sim\operatorname{Binomial}(N,p)\). If \(E[N]=10\), compute \(E[X]\).

Solution

Given \(N\), \(E[X\mid N]=Np\). Therefore \(E[X]=E[Np]=10p\).

4. Total variance with two groups
ConceptVariance

\(Y\) is 0 or 1 with equal probability. \(E[X\mid Y=0]=0\), \(E[X\mid Y=1]=6\), and \(\operatorname{Var}(X\mid Y)=2\) for both groups. Compute \(\operatorname{Var}(X)\).

Solution

\(E[\operatorname{Var}(X\mid Y)]=2\). The conditional mean is 0 or 6 with equal probability, so its variance is 9. Hence \(\operatorname{Var}(X)=11\).

5. Conditional support for a uniform point
ConceptSupport

Choose \(Y\sim\operatorname{Uniform}(0,1)\). Given \(Y=y\), choose \(X\sim\operatorname{Uniform}(0,y)\). Compute \(E[X]\).

Solution

Given \(Y=y\), \(E[X\mid Y=y]=y/2\). Therefore \(E[X]=E[Y/2]=1/4\).

Problems 6-8 - Application Problems
6. First die controls the second reward
ApplicationConditioning choice

Roll a fair die \(Y\). Then roll \(Y\) fair coins and let \(X\) be the number of heads. Compute \(E[X]\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(X)\).

Solution

Given \(Y\), \(X\sim\operatorname{Binomial}(Y,1/2)\). \(E[X\mid Y]=Y/2\), so \(E[X]=E[Y]/2=7/4\). Also \(E[\operatorname{Var}(X\mid Y)]=E[Y/4]=7/8\), and \(\operatorname{Var}(E[X\mid Y])=\operatorname{Var}(Y/2)=35/48\). Total variance is \(77/48\).

7. Draws until a red card count
ApplicationHypergeometric

A random hand size \(N\) has mean 5. Given \(N\), \(N\) cards are drawn from a large balanced deck with red probability \(1/2\). Let \(X\) be red cards. Approximate \(E[X]\) using conditioning.

Solution

Given \(N\), the conditional mean is \(N/2\). Therefore \(E[X]=E[N/2]=5/2\). The calculation uses only the conditional mean, not the full distribution of \(N\).

8. Mixed machine defect rate
ApplicationMixture

A part comes from machine A with probability \(0.7\) and B with probability \(0.3\). Defect probabilities are \(0.02\) and \(0.08\). Compute the defect probability.

Solution

Let \(Y\) be the machine and \(X\) the defect indicator. \(E[X\mid Y=A]=0.02\), \(E[X\mid Y=B]=0.08\). Thus \(P(\text{defect})=E[X]=0.7(0.02)+0.3(0.08)=0.038\).

Problems 9-10 - Hidden-Pattern Challenge
9. Random interval length
HiddenTower

Choose \(L\sim\operatorname{Uniform}(0,1)\). Given \(L\), throw \(n\) iid uniform points on \([0,1]\). Let \(X\) be the number falling in an interval of length \(L\). Compute \(E[X]\).

Solution

Given \(L\), \(X\sim\operatorname{Binomial}(n,L)\), so \(E[X\mid L]=nL\). Hence \(E[X]=nE[L]=n/2\).

10. Compound Poisson ticket total
HiddenRandom sum

\(N\sim\operatorname{Poisson}(\lambda)\). Given \(N\), rewards \(Z_1,\ldots,Z_N\) are iid with mean \(\mu\). Let \(S=\sum_{i=1}^N Z_i\). Compute \(E[S]\).

Solution

Given \(N\), \(E[S\mid N]=N\mu\). Therefore \(E[S]=E[N\mu]=\lambda\mu\).
