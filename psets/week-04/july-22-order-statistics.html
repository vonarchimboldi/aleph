Core Pattern

Turn an order-statistic event into a statement about how many sample values fall below a threshold.

\[P(X_{(k)}\le x)=P(\#\{i:X_i\le x\}\ge k)=\sum_{j=k}^n\binom nj F(x)^j(1-F(x))^{n-j}.\]

Setup Checklist

  • Decide whether the event concerns a minimum, maximum, kth value, or spacing.
  • Write the support of the ordered values.
  • Use the CDF-count method before differentiating.
  • For uniform samples, remember \(U_{(k)}\sim\operatorname{Beta}(k,n+1-k)\).
  • For joint laws, include the ordering region.
  • Check whether the answer is a probability, density, or expectation.

Problems 1-5 - Concept Builders
1. Concept: maximum below a threshold
ConceptMaximum

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_6\) be iid with CDF \(F\). Express \(P(X_{(6)}\le a)\).

Solution

The maximum is at most \(a\) exactly when all six observations are at most \(a\). Hence \(P(X_{(6)}\le a)=F(a)^6\).

2. Concept: minimum survival probability
ConceptMinimum

For iid observations with CDF \(F\), find \(P(X_{(1)}>a)\) for a sample of size \(n\).

Solution

The minimum exceeds \(a\) when every observation exceeds \(a\). Thus \(P(X_{(1)}>a)=[1-F(a)]^n\).

3. Concept: median event as binomial count
ConceptMedian

Five iid observations have CDF \(F\). Express \(P(X_{(3)}\le a)\) as a binomial-count probability.

Solution

At least three observations must be at most \(a\). Therefore \(P(X_{(3)}\le a)=\sum_{j=3}^{5}\binom5jF(a)^j[1-F(a)]^{5-j}\).

4. Concept: beta mean for a uniform order value
ConceptBeta

If \(U_1,\ldots,U_n\) are iid Uniform\((0,1)\), find \(E[U_{(k)}]\).

Solution

\(U_{(k)}\) has beta distribution with parameters \(k\) and \(n+1-k\). Its mean is \(k/(n+1)\).

7. Application: joint density of two ordered uniforms
ApplicationJoint

For \(n=5\) iid Uniform\((0,1)\), write the joint density of \(U_{(2)}=u\) and \(U_{(4)}=v\) for \(0

Solution

The general joint density is \(\frac{n!}{(i-1)!(j-i-1)!(n-j)!}u^{i-1}(v-u)^{j-i-1}(1-v)^{n-j}\). With \(n=5,i=2,j=4\), it is \(120u(v-u)(1-v)\).

8. Application: exponential minimum scaling
ApplicationMinimum

If \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) are iid Exponential\((\lambda)\), find the distribution of \(X_{(1)}\).

Solution

\(P(X_{(1)}>x)=P(X_1>x,\ldots,X_n>x)=e^{-n\lambda x}\) for \(x\ge0\). Thus \(X_{(1)}\sim\operatorname{Exponential}(n\lambda)\).
