Core Pattern

OLS chooses coefficients that make residuals orthogonal to every fitted direction.

\[\widehat\beta=(X^TX)^{-1}X^Ty,\qquad X^T(y-X\widehat\beta)=0,\qquad \widehat y=X\widehat\beta.\]

Setup Checklist

  • For simple regression, compute means first.
  • Use slope \(S_{xy}/S_{xx}\) when an intercept is present.
  • Find intercept from the mean point.
  • For matrix OLS, write the normal equation.
  • For residual questions, multiply by \(X^T\).
  • For projection questions, identify the column space.

Problems 1-5 - Concept Builders
1. Concept: simple-regression slope from sums
ConceptSlope

In a simple regression with intercept, suppose \(S_{xy}=20\) and \(S_{xx}=5\). Find the OLS slope.

Solution

The slope is \(b_1=S_{xy}/S_{xx}=20/5=4\).

2. Concept: intercept from the mean point
ConceptIntercept

If the fitted slope is \(3\), \(\bar x=2\), and \(\bar y=11\), find the intercept in a simple regression with intercept.

Solution

The fitted line passes through \((\bar x,\bar y)\). Thus \(b_0=\bar y-b_1\bar x=11-3\cdot2=5\).

3. Concept: residual sum with intercept
ConceptResidual

In OLS with an intercept column, what is \(\sum_i e_i\), where \(e_i=y_i-\widehat y_i\)?

Solution

The residual vector is orthogonal to the intercept column of ones. Therefore \(\sum_i e_i=0\).

4. Concept: normal equation recognition
ConceptNormal equation

For full-column-rank design matrix \(X\), write the normal equation that defines the OLS estimate of \(\beta\).

Solution

The normal equation is \(X^TX\widehat\beta=X^Ty\). With full column rank, \(X^TX\) is invertible and \(\widehat\beta=(X^TX)^{-1}X^Ty\).

5. Concept: projection matrix property
ConceptProjection

Let \(H=X(X^TX)^{-1}X^T\). State two algebraic properties of \(H\) when \(X\) has full column rank.

Solution

The hat matrix is symmetric, \(H^T=H\), and idempotent, \(H^2=H\). It projects \(y\) onto the column space of \(X\).

Problems 6-8 - Integration Problems
6. Integration: compute a fitted line
IntegrationMean

For points \((1,2),(2,3),(3,5)\), compute the OLS line with intercept.

Solution

\(\bar x=2\), \(\bar y=10/3\). \(S_{xy}=(-1)(-4/3)+0(-1/3)+1(5/3)=3\), and \(S_{xx}=2\). Thus \(b_1=3/2\), and \(b_0=10/3-(3/2)2=1/3\). The fitted line is \(\widehat y=1/3+(3/2)x\).

7. Integration: residual orthogonality proof
IntegrationOrthogonal

Assume \(X^TX\widehat\beta=X^Ty\). Show that the residual \(e=y-X\widehat\beta\) is orthogonal to every column of \(X\).

Solution

Compute \(X^Te=X^T(y-X\widehat\beta)=X^Ty-X^TX\widehat\beta=0\). A vector has zero inner product with every column of \(X\) exactly when \(X^Te=0\).

8. Integration: fitted mean identity
IntegrationFitted values

In simple regression with an intercept, show that the average fitted value equals \(\bar y\).

Solution

The fitted values are \(\widehat y_i=b_0+b_1x_i\). Averaging gives \(\overline{\widehat y}=b_0+b_1\bar x\). Since \(b_0=\bar y-b_1\bar x\), \(\overline{\widehat y}=\bar y\).

Problems 9-10 - ISI-Style Challenge
9. ISI-style challenge: slope invariance under shifting \(x\)
ISI-styleInvariance

In simple regression with intercept, each \(x_i\) is replaced by \(x_i+c\). Explain what happens to the slope and intercept.

Solution

The centered values \(x_i-\bar x\) do not change after adding \(c\), so \(S_{xy}\) and \(S_{xx}\) are unchanged. The slope stays the same. The new intercept becomes \(b_0-cb_1\) so that fitted values remain unchanged.

10. ISI-style challenge: projection error split
ISI-styleProjection

Let \(H\) be the OLS projection matrix and \(e=(I-H)y\). Show that \(\|y\|^2=\|Hy\|^2+\|e\|^2\).

Solution

\(Hy\) lies in the column space of \(X\), while \(e\) is orthogonal to that space. Therefore \((Hy)^Te=0\). Since \(y=Hy+e\), expanding the squared norm gives \(\|y\|^2=\|Hy\|^2+\|e\|^2\).
