Core Pattern

Condition on the variable that makes the remaining random quantity familiar.

\[E[X]=E(E[X\mid Y]),\qquad \operatorname{Var}(X)=E(\operatorname{Var}(X\mid Y))+\operatorname{Var}(E[X\mid Y]).\]

Setup Checklist

  • Name the variable being conditioned on.
  • Write the conditional distribution or conditional mean.
  • Check the possible values of the conditioning variable.
  • Average the inner answer over the outer distribution.
  • For random sums, condition on the number of terms.
  • For total variance, compute both pieces separately.

Problems 1-5 - Mechanics Builders
1. Concept mechanic: die then Bernoulli trials
ConceptTower

Roll a fair die \(N\), then run \(N\) independent Bernoulli\((p)\) trials. Let \(X\) be the number of successes. Compute \(E[X]\).

Solution

Given \(N\), \(E[X\mid N]=Np\). Therefore \(E[X]=pE[N]=p(7/2)\).

2. Concept mechanic: conditional mean from a two-stage box
ConceptConditioning

A coin with random success probability \(P\) is chosen, where \(P\) is equally likely to be \(1/4\) or \(3/4\). One toss is made and \(X\) is the success indicator. Find \(E[X]\).

Solution

Condition on \(P\). Since \(E[X\mid P]=P\), \(E[X]=E[P]=(1/4+3/4)/2=1/2\).

3. Concept mechanic: random count with known mean
ConceptRandom sum

Given \(N\), \(X_1,\ldots,X_N\) are iid with mean \(5\), independent of \(N\). If \(E[N]=12\), find \(E[\sum_{i=1}^N X_i]\).

Solution

Condition on \(N\). The inner mean is \(E[\sum_{i=1}^N X_i\mid N]=5N\). Averaging gives \(60\).

4. Concept mechanic: total variance with random coin
ConceptVariance

A Bernoulli trial has random probability \(P\), where \(P\) is equally likely to be \(1/3\) or \(2/3\). Let \(X\) be its outcome. Use total variance to compute \(\operatorname{Var}(X)\).

Solution

\(E[X\mid P]=P\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(X\mid P)=P(1-P)\). Thus \(E[\operatorname{Var}(X\mid P)]=2/9\), and \(\operatorname{Var}(E[X\mid P])=\operatorname{Var}(P)=1/36\). Total variance is \(2/9+1/36=1/4\).

5. Concept mechanic: support after observing a count
ConceptSupport

A box type \(Y\) is either A or B with equal probability. Type A has \(2\) red and \(2\) blue balls; type B has \(4\) red and \(1\) blue ball. One ball is drawn. Find the probability it is red by conditioning on \(Y\).

Solution

\(P(\text{red}\mid Y=A)=1/2\) and \(P(\text{red}\mid Y=B)=4/5\). Hence \(P(\text{red})=(1/2)(1/2)+(1/2)(4/5)=13/20\).

Problems 6-8 - Application Problems
6. Application: geometric number of inspections
ApplicationRandom sum

A machine is inspected until the first defect is found. The number of inspections \(N\) has geometric distribution with \(P(N=k)=(1-q)^{k-1}q\). Each inspection costs \(C_i\) with mean \(c\), independent of \(N\). Find the expected total cost.

Solution

Condition on \(N\). The conditional expected cost is \(cN\). Since \(E[N]=1/q\), the expected total cost is \(c/q\).

7. Application: posterior box expectation
ApplicationConditional distribution

A box is type A with probability \(1/3\) and type B with probability \(2/3\). Type A gives \(X\sim\operatorname{Binomial}(4,1/2)\); type B gives \(X\sim\operatorname{Binomial}(4,3/4)\). Compute \(E[X]\) by conditioning on the box type.

Solution

The conditional means are \(2\) and \(3\). Therefore \(E[X]=(1/3)2+(2/3)3=8/3\).

8. Application: variance of a random sum of indicators
ApplicationTotal variance

Given \(N\), \(X\mid N\sim\operatorname{Binomial}(N,p)\). Suppose \(E[N]=\mu\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(N)=\sigma^2\). Find \(\operatorname{Var}(X)\).

Solution

\(E[X\mid N]=Np\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(X\mid N)=Np(1-p)\). Thus \(\operatorname{Var}(X)=E[Np(1-p)]+\operatorname{Var}(Np)=\mu p(1-p)+p^2\sigma^2\).

Problems 9-10 - Hidden ISI-Style Challenge
9. Hidden ISI-style challenge: random maximum sample size
HiddenConditioning

Given \(N\), take \(N\) iid Uniform\((0,1)\) variables. Let \(M\) be their maximum, with \(M=0\) if \(N=0\). If \(N\sim\operatorname{Poisson}(\lambda)\), compute \(P(M\le x)\) for \(0\le x\le1\).

Solution

Condition on \(N\). Given \(N=n\), \(P(M\le x\mid N=n)=x^n\), including \(n=0\). Therefore \(P(M\le x)=E[x^N]=\exp(\lambda(x-1))\).

10. Hidden ISI-style challenge: conditional expectation through a stopping count
HiddenTower

A coin with head probability \(p\) is tossed until the second head appears. Let \(N\) be the total number of tosses. Compute the expected number of tails observed before the second head.

Solution

The number of tails before the second head is \(N-2\). For a negative-binomial count to get two heads, \(E[N]=2/p\). Hence the expected number of tails is \(2/p-2=2(1-p)/p\).
