Core Pattern

Maximum likelihood estimation has one repeated shape. The estimator is the parameter value that makes the observed sample most plausible.

\[ L(\theta)=\prod_{i=1}^{n} f_\theta(x_i), \qquad \ell(\theta)=\log L(\theta). \]

The log-likelihood is usually easier to optimize. But the allowed values of the parameter come first. A derivative answer outside the parameter space is not the MLE.

Setup Checklist

  • Write the model and the parameter space before calculating.
  • Write the product likelihood. Keep only factors that involve the parameter when optimizing.
  • Take logs after checking the likelihood is positive on the allowed parameter range.
  • Find interior critical points, then compare them with boundaries when boundaries exist.
  • For Bernoulli and binomial models, the MLE is usually a sample proportion.
  • For exponential models, decide whether the parameter is the rate or the mean.
  • For uniform support models, the support condition often determines the MLE.
  • After the answer, state what the estimator means in the original model.
Problems 1-5 · Concept Builders
1. Bernoulli Likelihood From Counts
Concept Product likelihood 7 min

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be independent Bernoulli\((p)\), where \(0

Solution

The joint probability of the observed sample is

\[ L(p)=\prod_{i=1}^n p^{x_i}(1-p)^{1-x_i}. \]

Collect the powers of \(p\) and \(1-p\):

\[ L(p)=p^s(1-p)^{n-s}. \]

The log-likelihood is

\[ \ell(p)=s\log p+(n-s)\log(1-p). \]

Differentiate and set equal to zero:

\[ \ell'(p)=\frac{s}{p}-\frac{n-s}{1-p}=0. \]

Solving gives \(s(1-p)=(n-s)p\), so \(s=np\). Hence

\[ \widehat p=\frac{s}{n}=\overline X. \]
2. Poisson Mean Setup
Concept Log-likelihood 8 min

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be independent Poisson\((\lambda)\), where \(\lambda>0\). Write the log-likelihood up to constants that do not depend on \(\lambda\), and find the MLE of \(\lambda\).

Solution

The joint likelihood is

\[ L(\lambda)=\prod_{i=1}^n e^{-\lambda}\frac{\lambda^{x_i}}{x_i!} =e^{-n\lambda}\lambda^{\sum_i x_i}\prod_{i=1}^n \frac{1}{x_i!}. \]

The factorial term does not involve \(\lambda\). The log-likelihood for optimizing is

\[ \ell(\lambda)=-n\lambda+\left(\sum_i x_i\right)\log\lambda + C. \]

Differentiate:

\[ \ell'(\lambda)=-n+\frac{\sum_i x_i}{\lambda}. \]

Set this equal to zero:

\[ \widehat\lambda=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n x_i=\overline X. \]
3. Exponential Rate, Not Mean
Concept Rate parameter 8 min

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be independent Exponential\((\lambda)\) with density \(f_\lambda(x)=\lambda e^{-\lambda x}\) for \(x\ge0\), \(\lambda>0\). Find the MLE of the rate \(\lambda\).

Solution

The likelihood is

\[ L(\lambda)=\prod_{i=1}^n \lambda e^{-\lambda x_i} =\lambda^n e^{-\lambda\sum_i x_i}. \]

The log-likelihood is

\[ \ell(\lambda)=n\log\lambda-\lambda\sum_i x_i. \]

Differentiate:

\[ \ell'(\lambda)=\frac{n}{\lambda}-\sum_i x_i. \]

The critical point is

\[ \widehat\lambda=\frac{n}{\sum_i x_i}=\frac{1}{\overline X}. \]

This estimates the rate. The estimated mean would be \(1/\widehat\lambda=\overline X\).

4. Normal Mean With Known Variance
Concept Quadratic loss 8 min

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be independent \(N(\mu,\sigma^2)\), where \(\sigma^2\) is known and \(\mu\in\mathbb R\). Show that the MLE of \(\mu\) is the sample mean.

Solution

The likelihood is proportional to

\[ \exp\left(-\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\sum_{i=1}^n (x_i-\mu)^2\right). \]

Maximizing this is the same as minimizing

\[ \sum_{i=1}^n (x_i-\mu)^2. \]

Differentiate this sum with respect to \(\mu\):

\[ \frac{d}{d\mu}\sum_i(x_i-\mu)^2=-2\sum_i(x_i-\mu). \]

Set the derivative equal to zero:

\[ \sum_i x_i-n\mu=0. \]

Thus

\[ \widehat\mu=\overline X. \]
5. Uniform Upper Endpoint
Concept Support check 9 min

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be independent Uniform\((0,\theta)\), where \(\theta>0\). Let \(m=\max_i x_i\). Write the likelihood and find the MLE of \(\theta\).

Solution

The density of one observation is \(1/\theta\) when \(0\le x_i\le\theta\). For the whole sample to be possible, \(\theta\) must be at least the largest observation \(m\).

The likelihood is

\[ L(\theta)=\theta^{-n}\mathbf 1\{\theta\ge m\}. \]

For \(\theta\ge m\), the value \(\theta^{-n}\) decreases as \(\theta\) increases. So the largest likelihood occurs at the smallest allowed value:

\[ \widehat\theta=m=X_{(n)}. \]
Problems 6-8 · Applications
6. Binomial Sample With Known Trial Size
Application Sample proportion 12 min

For each day \(i=1,\ldots,n\), \(X_i\sim\operatorname{Binomial}(r,p)\) independently, where \(r\) is known and \(0

Solution

The likelihood contains the factor

\[ \prod_{i=1}^n p^{x_i}(1-p)^{r-x_i}. \]

Ignoring binomial coefficients that do not depend on \(p\), this is

\[ L(p)\propto p^{\sum_i x_i}(1-p)^{nr-\sum_i x_i}. \]

This is the same shape as a Bernoulli likelihood with \(nr\) total trials and \(\sum_i x_i\) successes. Therefore

\[ \widehat p=\frac{\sum_{i=1}^n x_i}{nr}. \]
7. Geometric Probability
Application Support and log rules 14 min

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be independent geometric random variables with \(P(X=k)=p(1-p)^{k-1}\) for \(k=1,2,\ldots\), where \(0

Solution

The likelihood is

\[ L(p)=\prod_{i=1}^n p(1-p)^{x_i-1} =p^n(1-p)^{\sum_i x_i-n}. \]

The log-likelihood is

\[ \ell(p)=n\log p+\left(\sum_i x_i-n\right)\log(1-p). \]

Differentiate:

\[ \ell'(p)=\frac{n}{p}-\frac{\sum_i x_i-n}{1-p}. \]

Set this equal to zero:

\[ n(1-p)=p\left(\sum_i x_i-n\right). \]

So \(n=p\sum_i x_i\), and

\[ \widehat p=\frac{n}{\sum_i x_i}=\frac{1}{\overline X}. \]
8. Boundary From a Constrained Mean
Application Constraint 13 min

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be independent \(N(\mu,1)\), but suppose the model requires \(\mu\ge0\). Find the MLE of \(\mu\).

Solution

Without the constraint, the MLE is \(\overline X\). The parameter space is now \([0,\infty)\).

The log-likelihood is maximized by choosing the allowed \(\mu\) closest to \(\overline X\), because the likelihood depends on \(\sum_i(x_i-\mu)^2\).

If \(\overline X\ge0\), the unconstrained maximizer is allowed. If \(\overline X<0\), the nearest allowed value is the boundary \(0\). Therefore

\[ \widehat\mu=\max(0,\overline X). \]
Problems 9-10 · Hidden-Pattern / ISI Style
9. Shifted Uniform Support
Challenge Support-dependent 18 min

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be independent Uniform\((\theta,\theta+1)\), where \(\theta\in\mathbb R\). Let \(a=\min_i x_i\) and \(b=\max_i x_i\). Describe all allowed \(\theta\), then find the MLE.

Solution

For every observation to lie in \((\theta,\theta+1)\), we need

\[ \theta\le a \qquad\text{and}\qquad b\le\theta+1. \]

The second condition is \(\theta\ge b-1\). So the allowed interval is

\[ b-1\le\theta\le a. \]

Inside this interval, the likelihood is \(1\), because the density height is \(1\) for each observation. Outside this interval, the likelihood is \(0\).

Every \(\theta\) in the interval maximizes the likelihood. The MLE is not unique:

\[ \widehat\theta\in[b-1,a]. \]
10. Two-Parameter Normal Basics
ISI-style Mean and variance 20 min

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be independent \(N(\mu,\sigma^2)\), where both \(\mu\in\mathbb R\) and \(\sigma^2>0\) are unknown. State the MLEs of \(\mu\) and \(\sigma^2\), and explain why the variance MLE has denominator \(n\), not \(n-1\).

Solution

The likelihood is maximized over \(\mu\) at

\[ \widehat\mu=\overline X. \]

Plugging this into the likelihood leaves the variance parameter. The MLE is

\[ \widehat{\sigma^2}=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n (X_i-\overline X)^2. \]

The denominator is \(n\) because MLE maximizes likelihood. The denominator \(n-1\) belongs to the unbiased sample variance. MLE and unbiased estimation answer different questions.

Answer Summary

  1. \(\widehat p=s/n=\overline X\).
  2. \(\widehat\lambda=\overline X\) for Poisson mean.
  3. \(\widehat\lambda=n/\sum_i x_i=1/\overline X\) for exponential rate.
  4. \(\widehat\mu=\overline X\).
  5. \(\widehat\theta=X_{(n)}\).
  6. \(\widehat p=\sum_i x_i/(nr)\).
  7. \(\widehat p=n/\sum_i x_i=1/\overline X\).
  8. \(\widehat\mu=\max(0,\overline X)\).
  9. All \(\theta\in[b-1,a]\) are MLEs.
  10. \(\widehat\mu=\overline X\), \(\widehat{\sigma^2}=n^{-1}\sum_i(X_i-\overline X)^2\).
