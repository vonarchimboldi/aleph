Core Pattern

When the probability of one indicator changes with a hidden variable, condition first.

\[ X=\sum_i I_i,\qquad E[X\mid Y]=\sum_i P(I_i=1\mid Y), \qquad E[X]=E[E[X\mid Y]]. \]

For pair terms, condition first if it makes the pair probability easy:

\[ E[X(X-1)\mid Y]=\sum_{i\ne j}P(I_i=1,I_j=1\mid Y). \]

Recognition Checklist

  • What is being counted?
  • What is the yes/no event for one object?
  • Which hidden variable changes the event probability?
  • Does conditioning make the indicators symmetric?
  • Do I need only \(E[X]\), or a pair term too?
  • Can I compute \(E[X\mid Y]\) before \(E[X]\)?
  • Are pair indicators independent only after conditioning?
  • Is the full distribution more work than needed?
Problems 1-5 · Concept Builders
1. Coins After a Random Spinner
Concept Tower 6 min

A spinner chooses \(P\), where \(P=1/4\) with probability \(1/3\) and \(P=3/4\) with probability \(2/3\). Then \(10\) independent coins are tossed, each with head probability \(P\). Let \(H\) be the number of heads. Find \(E[H]\).

Solution

Let \(I_i=\mathbf{1}\{\text{coin }i\text{ is heads}\}\). Conditional on \(P\), each indicator has expectation \(P\). So

\[ E[H\mid P]=\sum_{i=1}^{10}E[I_i\mid P]=10P. \]

Now average over the spinner:

\[ E[H]=E[10P]=10E[P]. \]

Compute

\[ E[P]=\frac13\cdot\frac14+\frac23\cdot\frac34 =\frac{1}{12}+\frac12=\frac7{12}. \]

Therefore

\[ E[H]=10\cdot\frac7{12}=\frac{35}{6}. \]
2. Random Number of Customer Arrivals
Concept Random count 7 min

A shop receives \(N\) customers in a day, where \(E[N]=40\). Conditional on \(N\), each customer buys a notebook independently with probability \(0.3\). Let \(B\) be the number of notebook buyers. Find \(E[B]\).

Solution

Conditional on \(N=n\), write

\[ B=\sum_{i=1}^{n} I_i, \]

where \(I_i\) indicates that customer \(i\) buys a notebook. Then

\[ E[B\mid N=n]=0.3n. \]

Use the tower property:

\[ E[B]=E[E[B\mid N]]=E[0.3N]=0.3E[N]=12. \]
3. Defects With a Random Machine
Concept Mixture 8 min

A machine is chosen at random. Machine A is chosen with probability \(0.6\) and has defect probability \(0.02\). Machine B is chosen with probability \(0.4\) and has defect probability \(0.05\). After the machine is chosen, \(100\) items are made independently. Let \(D\) be the number of defective items. Find \(E[D]\).

Solution

Let \(M\) be the chosen machine. Conditional on \(M\), each item has the same defect probability. So

\[ E[D\mid M=A]=100(0.02)=2, \qquad E[D\mid M=B]=100(0.05)=5. \]

Average over the machine choice:

\[ E[D]=0.6(2)+0.4(5)=1.2+2=3.2. \]

The indicator method is behind this: \(D=\sum I_i\), but the success probability is easier after conditioning on \(M\).

4. Active Sensors After a Random Weather State
Concept Conditioning 8 min

There are \(n\) sensors. The weather state \(W\) is dry with probability \(0.7\) and wet with probability \(0.3\). Conditional on \(W\), sensors act independently. A sensor is active with probability \(0.1\) in dry weather and \(0.4\) in wet weather. Let \(A\) be the number of active sensors. Find \(E[A]\).

Solution

Let \(I_i=\mathbf{1}\{\text{sensor }i\text{ is active}\}\). Conditional on the weather,

\[ E[A\mid W=\text{dry}]=0.1n, \qquad E[A\mid W=\text{wet}]=0.4n. \]

Now average over the two weather states:

\[ E[A]=0.7(0.1n)+0.3(0.4n)=0.07n+0.12n=0.19n. \]
5. Matching Answers With a Random Key
Concept Symmetry 10 min

A test has \(m\) multiple-choice questions, each with \(4\) choices. A student guesses independently and uniformly. The answer key is chosen uniformly among the \(4^m\) possible keys, independently of the guesses. Let \(C\) be the number of correct answers. Find \(E[C]\) by conditioning on the answer key.

Solution

Let \(K\) be the answer key. Conditional on any fixed key \(K\), each guessed answer matches its key answer with probability \(1/4\). Define

\[ I_i=\mathbf{1}\{\text{question }i\text{ is correct}\}. \]

Then

\[ E[C\mid K]=\sum_{i=1}^{m}P(I_i=1\mid K)=\frac{m}{4}. \]

This value does not depend on \(K\), so

\[ E[C]=E[E[C\mid K]]=\frac{m}{4}. \]
Problems 6-8 · Integration Problems
6. Shared Random Success Probability
Integration Pair terms 15 min

A random variable \(P\) is chosen first, with \(E[P]=\mu\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(P)=\tau^2\). Conditional on \(P\), \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) are independent Bernoulli\((P)\) variables. Let \(S=X_1+\cdots+X_n\). Find \(E[S]\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(S)\).

Solution

Conditional on \(P\), \(S\) is binomial with parameters \(n\) and \(P\). Hence

\[ E[S\mid P]=nP, \qquad \operatorname{Var}(S\mid P)=nP(1-P). \]

The expectation is

\[ E[S]=E[nP]=n\mu. \]

Use total variance:

\[ \operatorname{Var}(S) =E[nP(1-P)]+\operatorname{Var}(nP). \]

Now \(E[P(1-P)]=E[P]-E[P^2]\). Since \(E[P^2]=\tau^2+\mu^2\),

\[ E[P(1-P)]=\mu-\tau^2-\mu^2. \]

Therefore

\[ \operatorname{Var}(S)=n(\mu-\mu^2-\tau^2)+n^2\tau^2 =n\mu(1-\mu)+n(n-1)\tau^2. \]

The extra term appears because all indicators share the same random \(P\).

7. Empty Tables After a Random Crowd
Integration Occupancy 16 min

A restaurant has \(r\) tables. A random number \(N\) of customers arrives. Conditional on \(N\), each customer independently chooses one of the \(r\) tables uniformly. Let \(E_r\) be the number of empty tables. Express \(E[E_r]\) in terms of the probability generating function \(G_N(s)=E[s^N]\).

Solution

Let \(I_j=\mathbf{1}\{\text{table }j\text{ is empty}\}\). Then \(E_r=\sum_{j=1}^{r}I_j\). Conditional on \(N=n\), a fixed table is empty if every customer chooses one of the other \(r-1\) tables:

\[ P(I_j=1\mid N=n)=\left(1-\frac1r\right)^n. \]

So

\[ E[E_r\mid N=n]=r\left(1-\frac1r\right)^n. \]

Average over \(N\):

\[ E[E_r] =rE\left[\left(1-\frac1r\right)^N\right] =rG_N\left(1-\frac1r\right). \]
8. Pair Collisions With a Random Number of Draws
Integration Pair indicators 18 min

There are \(k\) equally likely colors. A random number \(N\) of balls is drawn independently with replacement, where \(E[N(N-1)]<\infty\). Conditional on \(N\), let \(Y\) be the number of unordered pairs of drawn balls that have the same color. Find \(E[Y]\) in terms of \(E[N(N-1)]\) and \(k\).

Solution

Conditional on \(N=n\), index unordered pairs of positions \(\{a,b\}\), with \(1\le a \[ I_{ab}=\mathbf{1}\{\text{positions }a\text{ and }b\text{ have the same color}\}. \]

Then \(Y=\sum_{a \[ E[Y\mid N=n]=\binom n2\frac1k. \]

Average over \(N\):

\[ E[Y]=E\left[\binom N2\frac1k\right] =\frac{E[N(N-1)]}{2k}. \]
Problems 9-10 · Hidden-Pattern Problems
9. Hidden Indicators in a Random Study Group
Hidden Exchangeability 22 min

A class has \(a\) algebra students and \(b\) statistics students. First choose a random group size \(N\), where \(1\le N\le a+b\). Conditional on \(N=n\), choose \(n\) students uniformly without replacement. Let \(T\) be the number of unordered pairs inside the chosen group that contain one algebra student and one statistics student. Find \(E[T]\) in terms of \(E[N(N-1)]\), \(a\), and \(b\).

Solution

Conditional on \(N=n\), count pairs of positions in the chosen group. For each unordered position pair \(\{u,v\}\), let \(I_{uv}=1\) if one selected student in those two positions is algebra and the other is statistics.

For a fixed pair of positions, the two students are like two draws without replacement from the whole class. The probability of one algebra and one statistics student is

\[ \frac{a}{a+b}\frac{b}{a+b-1} +\frac{b}{a+b}\frac{a}{a+b-1} =\frac{2ab}{(a+b)(a+b-1)}. \]

Thus, conditional on \(N=n\),

\[ E[T\mid N=n] =\binom n2\frac{2ab}{(a+b)(a+b-1)}. \]

Average over \(N\):

\[ E[T] =\frac{E[N(N-1)]}{2}\cdot\frac{2ab}{(a+b)(a+b-1)} =\frac{ab\,E[N(N-1)]}{(a+b)(a+b-1)}. \]
10. Conditional Indicator Choice in a Screening Test
Hidden ISI style 25 min

A lab tests \(N\) samples, where \(E[N]=m\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(N)=v\). Conditional on \(N\), each sample is truly positive independently with probability \(p\). A truly positive sample is detected with probability \(s\), and a truly negative sample gives a false positive with probability \(f\). All detection outcomes are conditionally independent given the true statuses and \(N\). Let \(Z\) be the number of positive test results. Find \(E[Z]\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(Z)\).

Solution

For one sample, the unconditional positive-test probability after the true status is mixed out is

\[ q=ps+(1-p)f. \]

Conditional on \(N=n\), the \(n\) test-result indicators are independent Bernoulli\((q)\). Therefore

\[ E[Z\mid N=n]=nq, \qquad \operatorname{Var}(Z\mid N=n)=nq(1-q). \]

Use the tower property for the mean:

\[ E[Z]=E[nq]=mq. \]

Use total variance:

\[ \operatorname{Var}(Z) =E[Nq(1-q)]+\operatorname{Var}(Nq) =mq(1-q)+v q^2. \]

The main decision is to choose the final test-result indicators, not separate indicators for every true-status and test-status combination.
