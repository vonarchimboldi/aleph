Core Pattern

Contest algebra often asks you to make an expression factorable before doing arithmetic. Look for a missing term, a hidden square, or a product that can telescope.

\[a^4+4b^4=(a^2-2ab+2b^2)(a^2+2ab+2b^2),\qquad xy+x+y=(x+1)(y+1)-1.\]

One-Hour Workflow

  • Spend 8 minutes reading the identities.
  • Spend 30 minutes on Problems 1-6.
  • Spend 15 minutes on Problems 7-10.
  • Name the factor pattern before calculating.
  • Try adding and subtracting a useful term.
  • Journal each trigger phrase and transformation.
Problems 1-5 - Concept Builders
1. Difference of Cubes Trigger
ConceptCubes

Factor \(x^3-27\).

Solution

Use \(a^3-b^3=(a-b)(a^2+ab+b^2)\). Here \(a=x\) and \(b=3\), so \(x^3-27=(x-3)(x^2+3x+9)\).

2. Sum of Cubes Trigger
ConceptCubes

Factor \(8y^3+1\).

Solution

Use \(a^3+b^3=(a+b)(a^2-ab+b^2)\). With \(a=2y\) and \(b=1\), the factorization is \((2y+1)(4y^2-2y+1)\).

3. Sophie Germain Warmup
ConceptSophie Germain

Factor \(x^4+4\).

Solution

Recognize Sophie Germain with \(a=x\), \(b=1\). Then \(x^4+4=(x^2-2x+2)(x^2+2x+2)\).

4. SFFT Setup
ConceptSimon trick

Rewrite \(xy+x+y=14\) as a product equation.

Solution

Add 1 to both sides. Since \(xy+x+y+1=(x+1)(y+1)\), we get \((x+1)(y+1)=15\).

5. Telescoping Ratio
ConceptTelescoping

Simplify \(\frac{2}{1}\cdot\frac{3}{2}\cdot\frac{4}{3}\cdot\frac{5}{4}\).

Solution

Each denominator cancels the previous numerator. The product becomes \(5/1=5\). The useful cue is adjacent factors that share numerator and denominator terms.

Problems 6-8 - Pattern Integration
6. Integer Solutions by SFFT
IntegrationInteger factors

Find all positive integer pairs \((x,y)\) satisfying \(xy+2x+3y=23\).

Solution

Multiply the constant corrections: \(xy+2x+3y+6=29\), so \((x+3)(y+2)=29\). Since 29 is prime, positive possibilities give \(x+3=29,y+2=1\) or \(x+3=1,y+2=29\), neither has both \(x,y\) positive. Therefore there are no positive integer pairs.

7. Hidden Sophie Germain
IntegrationAdd subtract

Factor \(x^4+4y^4\).

Solution

Use Sophie Germain directly with \(a=x\) and \(b=y\). The factorization is \(x^4+4y^4=(x^2-2xy+2y^2)(x^2+2xy+2y^2)\).

8. Difference of Fourth Powers
IntegrationPower difference

Factor \(a^4-b^4\) completely over integers.

Solution

First use difference of squares: \(a^4-b^4=(a^2-b^2)(a^2+b^2)\). Then factor again: \((a-b)(a+b)(a^2+b^2)\).

Problems 9-10 - Challenge Problems
9. AIME-Style Factor Count
ChallengeSFFT

How many positive integer pairs \((x,y)\) satisfy \(xy+x+y=35\)?

Solution

Add 1 to get \((x+1)(y+1)=36\). Positive \(x,y\) require both factors at least 2. The positive divisors of 36 are \(1,2,3,4,6,9,12,18,36\). Excluding factor 1 leaves 7 ordered divisor choices for \(x+1\), so there are 7 ordered pairs.

10. Telescoping Product Challenge
ChallengeTelescoping

Simplify \(\prod_{k=2}^{10}\frac{k^2-1}{k^2}\).

Solution

Factor each term: \(\frac{k^2-1}{k^2}=\frac{(k-1)(k+1)}{k^2}=\frac{k-1}{k}\cdot\frac{k+1}{k}\). The product becomes \((1/2)(2/3)\cdots(9/10)\cdot(3/2)(4/3)\cdots(11/10)=\frac{1}{10}\cdot\frac{11}{2}=\frac{11}{20}\).
