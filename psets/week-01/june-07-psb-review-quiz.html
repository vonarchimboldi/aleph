1. Indicators: Empty Boxes and Pair Terms
ISI-style Indicators Subparts

Ten balls are independently placed uniformly into five labelled boxes. Let \(X\) be the number of empty boxes. Answer: (a) write \(X\) as a sum of indicators, (b) compute \(E[X]\), (c) compute \(E[X^2]\), and (d) state \(\operatorname{Var}(X)\).

Solution

Let \(I_j=1\) if box \(j\) is empty. Then \(X=\sum_{j=1}^5 I_j\). For one box, \(P(I_j=1)=(4/5)^{10}\), so \(E[X]=5(4/5)^{10}\).

For \(j\ne k\), both boxes \(j,k\) are empty when all balls choose one of the other three boxes, so \(P(I_jI_k=1)=(3/5)^{10}\). Therefore

\[ E[X^2]=\sum_jE[I_j]+2\sum_{jFinally, \(\operatorname{Var}(X)=E[X^2]-(E[X])^2\). The key feedback point is whether the pair term uses two boxes empty, not two boxes independently empty.

2. Conditional Expectation: Choose the First Variable
ISI-style Tower property Subparts

A box is chosen first: box A with probability \(2/3\), box B with probability \(1/3\). Box A has mean reward 8 and variance 4; box B has mean reward 2 and variance 1. Let \(X\) be the reward. Answer: (a) define the conditioning variable, (b) compute \(E[X]\), (c) compute \(\operatorname{Var}(E[X\mid Y])\), and (d) compute \(\operatorname{Var}(X)\).

Solution

Let \(Y\) be the chosen box. Then \(E[X\mid Y=A]=8\), \(E[X\mid Y=B]=2\). By total expectation,

\[ E[X]=(2/3)8+(1/3)2=6. \]

The conditional mean is 8 with probability \(2/3\) and 2 with probability \(1/3\), so

\[ \operatorname{Var}(E[X\mid Y])=(2/3)(8-6)^2+(1/3)(2-6)^2=8. \]

Also \(E[\operatorname{Var}(X\mid Y)]=(2/3)4+(1/3)1=3\). Hence \(\operatorname{Var}(X)=3+8=11\).

3. Order Statistics: Median of Uniforms
ISI-style Order statistics Subparts

Let \(U_1,\ldots,U_5\) be iid Uniform\((0,1)\), and let \(M=U_{(3)}\). Answer: (a) express \(P(M\le m)\) as a binomial count, (b) identify the beta distribution of \(M\), (c) compute \(E[M]\), and (d) compute \(P(M\le 1/2)\).

Solution

The event \(M\le m\) means at least three of the five uniforms are at most \(m\). Therefore

\[ P(M\le m)=\sum_{j=3}^5\binom5j m^j(1-m)^{5-j}. \]

For uniform order statistics, \(U_{(k)}\sim\operatorname{Beta}(k,n+1-k)\). Here \(M\sim\operatorname{Beta}(3,3)\), so \(E[M]=3/(3+3)=1/2\).

By symmetry of the median from five uniforms, \(P(M\le1/2)=1/2\). The binomial expression gives the same result: \(P(\operatorname{Binomial}(5,1/2)\ge3)=1/2\).

4. MLE: Support-Dependent Endpoint
ISI-style MLE Subparts

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be iid Uniform\((\theta,\theta+1)\). Answer: (a) write the likelihood support condition, (b) identify all \(\theta\) values that make the sample possible, (c) describe the MLE set, and (d) give one convention for reporting an MLE.

Solution

The density is 1 when \(\theta\le x_i\le\theta+1\), and 0 otherwise. All observations are possible exactly when

\[ \theta\le x_{(1)} \quad\text{and}\quad x_{(n)}\le\theta+1. \]

Thus \(\theta\le x_{(1)}\) and \(\theta\ge x_{(n)}-1\). The likelihood is constant, equal to 1, over the interval

\[ x_{(n)}-1\le\theta\le x_{(1)}. \]

Every point in that interval is an MLE. One reporting convention is \(\hat\theta=x_{(n)}-1\), the left endpoint. Another is the midpoint, but the important answer is the full MLE set.

5. UMP/NP: One-Sided Poisson Test
ISI-style UMP/NP Subparts

Let \(X_1,\ldots,X_n\) be iid Poisson\((\lambda)\). Test \(H_0:\lambda\le\lambda_0\) against \(H_1:\lambda>\lambda_0\). Answer: (a) find the likelihood ratio for \(\lambda_1>\lambda_0\), (b) identify the statistic, (c) state the UMP rejection form, and (d) explain how to calibrate size \(\alpha\).

Solution

Ignoring factors depending only on the data,

\[ L(\lambda)\propto e^{-n\lambda}\lambda^{\sum x_i}. \]

For \(\lambda_1>\lambda_0\),

\[ \frac{L(\lambda_1)}{L(\lambda_0)} = e^{-n(\lambda_1-\lambda_0)} \left(\frac{\lambda_1}{\lambda_0}\right)^{\sum x_i}. \]

This increases in \(T=\sum X_i\), so reject for large \(T\). Under the boundary null, \(T\sim\operatorname{Poisson}(n\lambda_0)\). Choose \(c\), and randomization at \(c\) if necessary, so the rejection probability under \(\lambda_0\) equals \(\alpha\).

6. Regression/OLS: Projection and Residuals
ISI-style Regression OLS Subparts

In the linear model \(y=X\beta+\varepsilon\), assume \(X\) has full column rank. Answer: (a) write the OLS estimator, (b) define the hat matrix, (c) prove the residual is orthogonal to each column of \(X\), and (d) state one consequence for fitted values and residuals.

Solution

The OLS estimator is

\[ \hat\beta=(X^\top X)^{-1}X^\top y. \]

The fitted vector is \(\hat y=X\hat\beta=Hy\), where \(H=X(X^\top X)^{-1}X^\top\). The residual is \(e=y-\hat y=(I-H)y\).

The normal equations give \(X^\top(y-X\hat\beta)=0\), so \(X^\top e=0\). This means the residual vector is orthogonal to every column of \(X\).

One consequence is that fitted values and residuals are orthogonal: \(\hat y^\top e=0\), because \(\hat y\) lies in the column space of \(X\).
