Core Pattern

For a monic polynomial with roots \(r_1,\ldots,r_n\),

\[ \prod_{i=1}^n (x-r_i) =x^n-e_1x^{n-1}+e_2x^{n-2}-\cdots+(-1)^ne_n, \]

where \(e_k\) is the sum of all products of \(k\) distinct roots. In contest problems, the useful move is usually not to expand. First ask which symmetric expression is needed.

One-Hour Workflow

  • Spend 8 minutes reviewing the pattern and examples.
  • Spend 30 minutes on Problems 1-6.
  • Spend 15 minutes on Problems 7-10.
  • Write the root sums before substituting numbers.
  • Use \(P(a)\), \(P(-a)\), or \(P(0)\) when roots are shifted or reciprocated.
  • Add one technique-journal entry for every missed trigger.

Fast Examples

If \(a,b\) are roots of \(x^2-5x+6=0\), then \(a+b=5\), \(ab=6\), and

\[ a^2+b^2=(a+b)^2-2ab=25-12=13. \]

If \(r_1,r_2,r_3\) are roots of \(x^3-2x^2-5x+7=0\), then

\[ r_1+r_2+r_3=2,\qquad r_1r_2+r_1r_3+r_2r_3=-5,\qquad r_1r_2r_3=-7. \]
Problems 1-5 · Concept Builders
1. Sum of Squares of Roots
Concept Vieta 5 min

Let \(a,b\) be the roots of \(x^2-7x+10=0\). Find \(a^2+b^2\).

Solution

By Vieta, \(a+b=7\) and \(ab=10\). Therefore

\[ a^2+b^2=(a+b)^2-2ab=49-20=29. \]
2. Reciprocals of Roots
Concept Reciprocal roots 6 min

Let \(a,b,c\) be the roots of \(2x^3-3x^2-5x+6=0\). Find \(\frac1a+\frac1b+\frac1c\).

Solution

For \(Ax^3+Bx^2+Cx+D\), the pairwise sum is \(C/A\) and the product is \(-D/A\). Here

\[ ab+bc+ca=\frac{-5}{2},\qquad abc=-\frac{6}{2}=-3. \]

Thus

\[ \frac1a+\frac1b+\frac1c=\frac{ab+bc+ca}{abc} =\frac{-5/2}{-3}=\frac56. \]
3. Build the Polynomial
Concept Symmetric sums 7 min

A monic cubic has roots \(p,q,r\) satisfying \(p+q+r=4\), \(pq+pr+qr=-1\), and \(pqr=-6\). Write the polynomial.

Solution

The monic cubic with roots \(p,q,r\) is

\[ x^3-(p+q+r)x^2+(pq+pr+qr)x-pqr. \]

Substitute the given values:

\[ x^3-4x^2-x+6. \]
4. Shifted Roots
Concept Transform roots 8 min

The roots of \(x^2-6x+5=0\) are \(a,b\). Find the monic polynomial whose roots are \(a+2\) and \(b+2\).

Solution

The original roots satisfy \(a+b=6\), \(ab=5\). The new roots have sum

\[ (a+2)+(b+2)=10 \]

and product

\[ (a+2)(b+2)=ab+2(a+b)+4=5+12+4=21. \]

So the polynomial is

\[ x^2-10x+21. \]
5. Evaluate Before Factoring
Concept Factor theorem 8 min

For \(P(x)=x^3-4x^2+x+6\), show that \(x-2\) is a factor and factor \(P(x)\) completely.

Solution

By the factor theorem, \(x-2\) is a factor exactly when \(P(2)=0\). Compute

\[ P(2)=8-16+2+6=0. \]

Divide by \(x-2\):

\[ x^3-4x^2+x+6=(x-2)(x^2-2x-3)=(x-2)(x-3)(x+1). \]
Problems 6-8 · Pattern Integration
6. Cubic Power Sum
Integration Power sums 12 min

Let \(a,b,c\) be the roots of \(x^3-3x^2+2x-5=0\). Find \(a^2+b^2+c^2\).

Solution

By Vieta,

\[ a+b+c=3,\qquad ab+bc+ca=2. \]

Then

\[ a^2+b^2+c^2=(a+b+c)^2-2(ab+bc+ca)=9-4=5. \]
7. Polynomial from Reciprocal Roots
Integration Root transform 14 min

The roots of \(2x^3-x^2-5x+2=0\) are \(a,b,c\). Find a polynomial whose roots are \(\frac1a,\frac1b,\frac1c\).

Solution

Use the transformation \(x=1/y\). If \(a\) is a root of \(P(x)=2x^3-x^2-5x+2\), then \(y=1/a\) satisfies

\[ P(1/y)=\frac{2}{y^3}-\frac{1}{y^2}-\frac5y+2=0. \]

Multiply by \(y^3\):

\[ 2-y-5y^2+2y^3=0. \]

So one polynomial is

\[ 2y^3-5y^2-y+2. \]
8. Hidden Integer Root
Integration Rational root theorem 15 min

Factor \(x^3+x^2-14x-24\) over the integers.

Solution

The rational root theorem says integer roots must divide \(24\). Try small divisors. Since

\[ P(4)=64+16-56-24=0, \]

\(x-4\) is a factor. Divide:

\[ x^3+x^2-14x-24=(x-4)(x^2+5x+6)=(x-4)(x+2)(x+3). \]
Problems 9-10 · Challenge
9. AIME-Style Symmetric Expression
Challenge Vieta synthesis 18 min

Let \(a,b\) be the roots of \(x^2-9x+4=0\). Find \(a^3+b^3\).

Solution

By Vieta, \(a+b=9\), \(ab=4\). Use

\[ a^3+b^3=(a+b)^3-3ab(a+b). \]

Therefore

\[ a^3+b^3=9^3-3\cdot4\cdot9=729-108=621. \]
10. Roots Shifted and Multiplied
Challenge Transform roots 20 min

The roots of \(x^2-5x+2=0\) are \(a,b\). Find the monic polynomial whose roots are \(2a-1\) and \(2b-1\).

Solution

The original roots satisfy \(a+b=5\), \(ab=2\). The transformed roots have sum

\[ (2a-1)+(2b-1)=2(a+b)-2=8. \]

The product is

\[ (2a-1)(2b-1)=4ab-2(a+b)+1=8-10+1=-1. \]

So the required monic polynomial is

\[ x^2-8x-1. \]
