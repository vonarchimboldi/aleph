Core Pattern

If \(X\) counts objects with some property, look for indicators \(I_i=\mathbf{1}_{A_i}\) such that

\[ X=\sum_i I_i,\qquad E[X]=\sum_i E[I_i]=\sum_i P(A_i). \]

Pattern Checklist

  • What object is being counted?
  • What is the right index set?
  • What event makes one indicator equal to \(1\)?
  • Are all indicators symmetric?
  • Do I need expectation only, or also variance?
  • For variance, which pairs overlap?
  • Can I condition first and then use indicators?
  • Is the full distribution unnecessary?
Problems 1-5 · Concept Builders
1. Marked Rolls
Concept Linearity 5 min

A fair die is rolled \(n\) times. Let \(X\) be the number of rolls whose value is either \(1\) or \(6\). Find \(E[X]\) using indicators.

Solution

Let \(I_i=\mathbf{1}\{\text{roll }i\text{ is }1\text{ or }6\}\). Then \(X=\sum_{i=1}^n I_i\), and

\[ E[I_i]=P(I_i=1)=\frac{2}{6}=\frac13. \]

Therefore

\[ E[X]=\sum_{i=1}^n E[I_i]=\frac{n}{3}. \]
2. Fixed Points in a Random Permutation
Concept Permutation 6 min

A permutation \(\sigma\) of \(\{1,2,\ldots,n\}\) is chosen uniformly at random. Let \(F\) be the number of fixed points, meaning positions \(i\) such that \(\sigma(i)=i\). Find \(E[F]\).

Solution

Let \(I_i=\mathbf{1}\{\sigma(i)=i\}\). Then \(F=\sum_{i=1}^n I_i\). For each fixed \(i\), symmetry gives

\[ P(\sigma(i)=i)=\frac1n. \]

Hence

\[ E[F]=\sum_{i=1}^n \frac1n=1. \]

The indicators are dependent, but independence is not needed for linearity of expectation.

3. Occupied Boxes
Concept Occupancy 8 min

Throw \(m\) balls independently and uniformly into \(n\) boxes. Let \(B\) be the number of boxes that receive at least one ball. Find \(E[B]\).

Solution

Let \(I_j=\mathbf{1}\{\text{box }j\text{ is nonempty}\}\). Then \(B=\sum_{j=1}^n I_j\). A fixed box is empty with probability \((1-1/n)^m\), so

\[ E[I_j]=P(I_j=1)=1-\left(1-\frac1n\right)^m. \]

Therefore

\[ E[B]=n\left[1-\left(1-\frac1n\right)^m\right]. \]
4. Adjacent Equal Letters
Concept Dependence 8 min

A random word \(W_1W_2\cdots W_n\) is formed by choosing each letter independently and uniformly from \(\{A,B,C,D\}\). Let \(A_n\) be the number of adjacent pairs \(i,i+1\) with \(W_i=W_{i+1}\). Find \(E[A_n]\).

Solution

Let \(I_i=\mathbf{1}\{W_i=W_{i+1}\}\) for \(1\le i\le n-1\). Then \(A_n=\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}I_i\). For a fixed adjacent pair, once \(W_i\) is chosen, \(W_{i+1}\) matches it with probability \(1/4\). Thus

\[ E[A_n]=(n-1)\frac14=\frac{n-1}{4}. \]

The adjacent-pair indicators overlap, but expectation still adds linearly.

5. Variance of Fixed Points
Concept Variance 12 min

Continue Problem 2. For a uniformly random permutation of \(\{1,\ldots,n\}\), with \(n\ge 2\), let \(F\) be the number of fixed points. Find \(\operatorname{Var}(F)\).

Solution

Use \(F=\sum_{i=1}^n I_i\), where \(I_i=\mathbf{1}\{\sigma(i)=i\}\). We already know \(E[F]=1\). Now compute \(E[F^2]\):

\[ F^2=\sum_i I_i + 2\sum_{iFor each \(i\), \(E[I_i]=1/n\). For \(i\ne j\),

\[ E[I_iI_j]=P(\sigma(i)=i,\sigma(j)=j)=\frac{(n-2)!}{n!}=\frac{1}{n(n-1)}. \]

Therefore

\[ E[F^2] =n\cdot\frac1n+2\binom{n}{2}\frac{1}{n(n-1)} =1+1=2. \]

Thus

\[ \operatorname{Var}(F)=E[F^2]-(E[F])^2=2-1=1. \]
Problems 6-8 · Pattern Integration
6. Empty Boxes: Second Moment
Integration Overlap pairs 15 min

Throw \(m\) balls independently and uniformly into \(n\) boxes, where \(n\ge 2\). Let \(E_m\) be the number of empty boxes. Find \(E[E_m]\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(E_m)\).

Solution

Let \(I_j=\mathbf{1}\{\text{box }j\text{ is empty}\}\). Then \(E_m=\sum_{j=1}^n I_j\). For a fixed box,

\[ p_1=P(I_j=1)=\left(1-\frac1n\right)^m. \]

So

\[ E[E_m]=np_1. \]

For two distinct boxes \(j\ne k\), both are empty iff every ball avoids both boxes:

\[ p_2=P(I_jI_k=1)=\left(1-\frac2n\right)^m. \]

Using \(E[E_m^2]=\sum_j E[I_j]+2\sum_{j \[ E[E_m^2]=np_1+n(n-1)p_2. \]

Therefore

\[ \operatorname{Var}(E_m)=np_1+n(n-1)p_2-n^2p_1^2. \]
7. Red Draws in Polya's Urn
Integration Exchangeability 15 min

An urn initially contains \(r\) red balls and \(b\) black balls. A ball is drawn, its color is recorded, and then it is returned together with \(c\) additional balls of the same color. This is repeated for \(n\) draws. Let \(R_n\) be the number of red draws. Find \(E[R_n]\).

Solution

Let \(I_k=\mathbf{1}\{\text{draw }k\text{ is red}\}\). Then

\[ R_n=\sum_{k=1}^n I_k. \]

In Polya's urn, the draw colors are exchangeable. In particular, every draw has the same marginal probability of being red as the first draw:

\[ P(I_k=1)=P(I_1=1)=\frac{r}{r+b}. \]

Therefore

\[ E[R_n]=\sum_{k=1}^n E[I_k]=\frac{nr}{r+b}. \]

The reinforcement creates dependence between draws, but linearity of expectation and exchangeability still give the expectation immediately.

8. Defectives After a Random Batch Size
Integration Conditioning 18 min

A factory produces \(N\) items in a day, where \(N\) is a nonnegative integer-valued random variable with \(E[N]<\infty\). Conditional on \(N=n\), each of the \(n\) items is defective independently with probability \(p\). Let \(D\) be the number of defective items. Use indicators conditional on \(N\) to find \(E[D]\) and \(\operatorname{Var}(D)\) in terms of \(E[N]\), \(\operatorname{Var}(N)\), and \(p\).

Solution

Conditional on \(N=n\), write \(D=\sum_{i=1}^n I_i\), where \(I_i\) indicates that item \(i\) is defective. Then

\[ E[D\mid N=n]=np,\qquad \operatorname{Var}(D\mid N=n)=np(1-p). \]

By the tower property,

\[ E[D]=E[E[D\mid N]]=E[Np]=pE[N]. \]

By total variance,

\[ \operatorname{Var}(D)=E[\operatorname{Var}(D\mid N)]+\operatorname{Var}(E[D\mid N]). \]

Thus

\[ \operatorname{Var}(D)=E[Np(1-p)]+\operatorname{Var}(Np) =p(1-p)E[N]+p^2\operatorname{Var}(N). \]

10. ISI PSB 2022: Children Receiving No Books

Source: ISI MStat PSB 2022, occupancy/covariance problem, reconstructed from the PSB prep guide.

ISI Past year 25 min

There are \(n\ge 2\) distinct books and \(n\) children. Each book is independently given to one of the \(n\) children, uniformly at random. Let \(X\) be the number of children who receive no books.

  1. Find \(E[X]\).
  2. Find \(\operatorname{Var}(X)\).
  3. Find the limiting value of \(E[X]/n\) as \(n\to\infty\).
Solution

Let \(I_i=\mathbf{1}\{\text{child }i\text{ receives no books}\}\). Then

\[ X=\sum_{i=1}^n I_i. \]

A fixed child receives no books if every book goes to one of the other \(n-1\) children, so

\[ p_1=P(I_i=1)=\left(\frac{n-1}{n}\right)^n. \]

Thus

\[ E[X]=np_1=n\left(1-\frac1n\right)^n. \]

For the variance, use the second moment. If \(i\ne j\), both children \(i\) and \(j\) receive no books iff every book goes to one of the remaining \(n-2\) children:

\[ p_2=P(I_iI_j=1)=\left(\frac{n-2}{n}\right)^n. \]

Now

\[ E[X^2]=\sum_iE[I_i]+2\sum_{iTherefore

\[ \operatorname{Var}(X) =np_1+n(n-1)p_2-n^2p_1^2. \]

Finally,

\[ \frac{E[X]}{n}=\left(1-\frac1n\right)^n\longrightarrow e^{-1}. \]
