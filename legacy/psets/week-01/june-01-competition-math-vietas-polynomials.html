Core Pattern

A polynomial has roots. Vieta's formulas connect those roots to the coefficients of the polynomial.

For a monic polynomial with roots \(r_1,\ldots,r_n\), we have

\[ \prod_{i=1}^n (x-r_i) =x^n-e_1x^{n-1}+e_2x^{n-2}-\cdots+(-1)^ne_n, \]

Here \(e_k\) means: add all products made from \(k\) different roots. This notation looks compact, but the idea is simple. Coefficients tell us the sum of the roots, the sum of pairwise products, and so on.

In contest problems, the useful move is usually not to expand. First ask: which sum or product of roots do I need?

One-Hour Workflow

  • Spend 8 minutes reading the pattern and examples slowly.
  • Spend 30 minutes on Problems 1-6.
  • Spend 15 minutes on Problems 7-10.
  • Before calculating, write what Vieta gives you.
  • If roots are changed, find the new sum and new product.
  • After solving, write one journal note for every missed idea.

Fast Examples

Start with the easiest case. If \(a,b\) are roots of \(x^2-5x+6=0\), then Vieta gives two facts:

\[ a+b=5,\qquad ab=6. \]

Now use those facts to find \(a^2+b^2\):

\[ a^2+b^2=(a+b)^2-2ab=25-12=13. \]

For a cubic, Vieta gives three facts. If \(r_1,r_2,r_3\) are roots of \(x^3-2x^2-5x+7=0\), then

\[ r_1+r_2+r_3=2,\qquad r_1r_2+r_1r_3+r_2r_3=-5,\qquad r_1r_2r_3=-7. \]
Problems 1-5 · Concept Builders
1. Sum of Squares of Roots
Concept Vieta 5 min

Let \(a,b\) be the roots of \(x^2-7x+10=0\). Find \(a^2+b^2\).

Solution

Use Vieta because the question asks about the roots, but it does not ask us to find the roots separately.

For \(x^2-7x+10=0\), Vieta gives

\[ a+b=7,\qquad ab=10. \]

Now rewrite \(a^2+b^2\) using the sum and product:

\[ a^2+b^2=(a+b)^2-2ab=49-20=29. \]

So the answer is \(29\).

2. Reciprocals of Roots
Concept Reciprocal roots 6 min

Let \(a,b,c\) be the roots of \(2x^3-3x^2-5x+6=0\). Find \(\frac1a+\frac1b+\frac1c\).

Solution

The expression \(\frac1a+\frac1b+\frac1c\) has a common denominator:

\[ \frac1a+\frac1b+\frac1c=\frac{ab+bc+ca}{abc}. \]

So we need two Vieta quantities: \(ab+bc+ca\) and \(abc\).

For \(2x^3-3x^2-5x+6=0\), Vieta gives

\[ ab+bc+ca=\frac{-5}{2},\qquad abc=-\frac{6}{2}=-3. \]

Substitute these values:

\[ \frac1a+\frac1b+\frac1c=\frac{ab+bc+ca}{abc} =\frac{-5/2}{-3}=\frac56. \]
3. Build the Polynomial
Concept Symmetric sums 7 min

A monic cubic has roots \(p,q,r\) satisfying \(p+q+r=4\), \(pq+pr+qr=-1\), and \(pqr=-6\). Write the polynomial.

Solution

Use the standard cubic form. A monic cubic with roots \(p,q,r\) is

\[ x^3-(p+q+r)x^2+(pq+pr+qr)x-pqr. \]

Now substitute the three given values:

\[ x^3-4x^2-x+6. \]
4. Shifted Roots
Concept Transform roots 8 min

The roots of \(x^2-6x+5=0\) are \(a,b\). Find the monic polynomial whose roots are \(a+2\) and \(b+2\).

Solution

First write what Vieta gives for the old roots:

\[ a+b=6,\qquad ab=5. \]

The new roots are \(a+2\) and \(b+2\). Their sum is

\[ (a+2)+(b+2)=10 \]

Their product is

\[ (a+2)(b+2)=ab+2(a+b)+4=5+12+4=21. \]

A monic quadratic with root sum \(10\) and root product \(21\) is

\[ x^2-10x+21. \]
5. Evaluate Before Factoring
Concept Factor theorem 8 min

For \(P(x)=x^3-4x^2+x+6\), show that \(x-2\) is a factor and factor \(P(x)\) completely.

Solution

The factor theorem says: \(x-2\) is a factor exactly when \(P(2)=0\).

So we check \(P(2)\):

\[ P(2)=8-16+2+6=0. \]

Since this is \(0\), \(x-2\) is a factor. Now divide by \(x-2\):

\[ x^3-4x^2+x+6=(x-2)(x^2-2x-3)=(x-2)(x-3)(x+1). \]
Problems 6-8 · Pattern Integration
6. Cubic Power Sum
Integration Power sums 12 min

Let \(a,b,c\) be the roots of \(x^3-3x^2+2x-5=0\). Find \(a^2+b^2+c^2\).

Solution

Again, we do not need the roots separately. We only need their sum and pairwise product sum. Vieta gives

\[ a+b+c=3,\qquad ab+bc+ca=2. \]

Now rewrite the expression using those two quantities:

\[ a^2+b^2+c^2=(a+b+c)^2-2(ab+bc+ca)=9-4=5. \]
7. Polynomial from Reciprocal Roots
Integration Root transform 14 min

The roots of \(2x^3-x^2-5x+2=0\) are \(a,b,c\). Find a polynomial whose roots are \(\frac1a,\frac1b,\frac1c\).

Solution

We want the reciprocal roots. Let \(y=1/a\). Then \(a=1/y\).

So we substitute \(x=1/y\) into the original polynomial:

\[ P(1/y)=\frac{2}{y^3}-\frac{1}{y^2}-\frac5y+2=0. \]

Now multiply by \(y^3\) to remove denominators:

\[ 2-y-5y^2+2y^3=0. \]

So one polynomial with the reciprocal roots is

\[ 2y^3-5y^2-y+2. \]
8. Hidden Integer Root
Integration Rational root theorem 15 min

Factor \(x^3+x^2-14x-24\) over the integers.

Solution

The rational root theorem gives a short list of possible integer roots. Any integer root must divide \(24\).

Try small divisors first. We find

\[ P(4)=64+16-56-24=0, \]

So \(x-4\) is a factor. Divide by \(x-4\):

\[ x^3+x^2-14x-24=(x-4)(x^2+5x+6)=(x-4)(x+2)(x+3). \]
Problems 9-10 · Challenge
9. AIME-Style Symmetric Expression
Challenge Vieta synthesis 18 min

Let \(a,b\) be the roots of \(x^2-9x+4=0\). Find \(a^3+b^3\).

Solution

Vieta gives

\[ a+b=9,\qquad ab=4. \]

The expression \(a^3+b^3\) can be rewritten using \(a+b\) and \(ab\):

\[ a^3+b^3=(a+b)^3-3ab(a+b). \]

Now substitute:

\[ a^3+b^3=9^3-3\cdot4\cdot9=729-108=621. \]
10. Roots Shifted and Multiplied
Challenge Transform roots 20 min

The roots of \(x^2-5x+2=0\) are \(a,b\). Find the monic polynomial whose roots are \(2a-1\) and \(2b-1\).

Solution

First write the old root information:

\[ a+b=5,\qquad ab=2. \]

The new roots are \(2a-1\) and \(2b-1\). Their sum is

\[ (2a-1)+(2b-1)=2(a+b)-2=8. \]

Their product is

\[ (2a-1)(2b-1)=4ab-2(a+b)+1=8-10+1=-1. \]

A monic quadratic with root sum \(8\) and root product \(-1\) is

\[ x^2-8x-1. \]
